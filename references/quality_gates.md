# Quality Gates for Universal Study Review

## Global gates

- The output identifies the task stage before doing the work.
- The output uses provided course materials before outside knowledge when materials exist.
- No unverified page numbers, chapter names, formulas, or teacher requirements are invented.
- Missing evidence is stated explicitly.
- Long outputs remain navigable through headings, tables, or checklists.

## S01 Material index gate

- Every provided file is listed.
- Each file has type, role, structure, and extraction limitations.
- Source paths or stable identifiers are preserved exactly as provided.
- Image/scanned content is marked as requiring visual verification.

## S03 Module breakdown gate

- Each module links back to specific sources or provided notes.
- Formulas include applicability conditions.
- Important examples and typical question types are separated from general explanation.
- Teacher/exam priority is not inferred unless supported by notes or rubrics.

## S08 Problem solution gate

- Known conditions and requested result are stated first.
- The chosen method is justified.
- Each transformation, theorem, formula, or boundary condition is named.
- The final answer includes required domains, conditions, units, intervals, regions of convergence, or assumptions when relevant.
- Pitfalls and checks are included.

## S10 Quick-review gate

- Content is compressed but not vague.
- The sheet distinguishes memorization items, method items, and warning items.
- It contains no unsupported “high-frequency” claims unless those came from teacher notes, past papers, or exam instructions.

## Iteration gate

- New materials update the index before downstream summaries are updated.
- Repeated mistakes are promoted into explicit rules or checklist items.
- Prompt templates are revised only after the failure pattern is clear.
